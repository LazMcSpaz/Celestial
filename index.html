Retrograde Explained

A planet in retrograde appears to move backward in the sky from Earth's perspective. This optical illusion occurs when Earth overtakes a slower-moving planet in their respective orbits around the Sun.

Retrograde periods are traditionally associated with reviewing, revisiting, and reconsidering — rather than initiating new actions.

The most well-known is Mercury Retrograde, which occurs 3–4 times per year for about three weeks each time.
